# AdventureWorks Wholesale Customer Analysis

## Project Overview

This project analyzes wholesale customer activity, revenue concentration, product performance, and regional differences using the Microsoft AdventureWorks sample database.

The goal was to understand:

* how customer activity changed over time
* which territories generated the most revenue
* where inactive customers were concentrated
* how much revenue depended on top customers
* which product categories drove order volume and revenue
* what actions could help retain, reactivate, and grow wholesale accounts

The analysis was completed in Power BI using calculated measures, customer segmentation, and interactive visualizations.

---

## Business Questions

This project focused on several business questions:

1. How many wholesale customers are Active, At-Risk, or Inactive?
2. Which territories generate the most revenue?
3. Which regions have the highest share of inactive customers?
4. How much revenue is generated by the top wholesale customers?
5. What are the characteristics of the top active and inactive accounts?
6. Which product categories drive order volume and revenue?
7. How does average order value differ by customer status?
8. What actions could help improve retention and revenue?

## Customer Status Definitions

Wholesale customers were grouped according to the timing of their most recent order:

* **Active** — customers who ordered recently
* **At-Risk** — customers whose order activity began to decline
* **Inactive** — customers who had not ordered for an extended period

At the end of the dataset, the wholesale customer base included:

| Customer status | Number of customers |
| --------------- | ------------------: |
| Active          |                 449 |
| At-Risk         |                  18 |
| Inactive        |                 168 |

Both the number of active customers and the number of inactive customers increased over time.

## Key Findings

### 1. Territory performance

The highest-revenue territories were:

1. Southwest
2. Canada
3. Northwest

Canada and Northwest had a lower share of inactive customers than Southwest.

The Southeast showed the greatest retention risk, with approximately **28% of wholesale customers classified as inactive**.

---

### 2. Top active customers

The Top 10 Active customers generated approximately **9.84% of total wholesale revenue**.

Key characteristics:

* each customer placed approximately 8–12 orders
* many became customers during the first year of the dataset
* Bikes were their main revenue-driving product category
* six of the Top 10 were located on the West Coast or in Canada

This geographic concentration was consistent with the overall territory revenue results.

---

### 3. Top inactive customers

The Top 10 Inactive customers accounted for approximately **5% of total wholesale revenue**.

Key characteristics:

* each customer placed approximately 4–8 orders
* most stopped ordering one to two years before the end of the dataset
* six were located along the southern coasts of the United States
* Southeast and Southwest had the highest percentages of inactive customers

These accounts represent meaningful reactivation opportunities because they previously generated substantial revenue.

---

### 4. Product category performance

Accessories and Components represented most wholesale orders.

Bikes accounted for a smaller share of total order volume but generated a much larger share of revenue.

This indicates that Bikes had a very high average order value and that wholesale revenue was sensitive to relatively small changes in Bike order volume.

---

### 5. Average order value

Average Order Value differed by customer status:

* Inactive customers had the highest AOV
* Active customers also had a strong AOV
* At-Risk customers had a lower AOV

This may suggest that order size starts to decrease before a customer becomes fully inactive.

It also shows that some inactive customers previously placed large orders, making them important targets for reactivation.

## Main Insights

### Top customers have a meaningful impact

The Top 10 Active customers generated approximately **9.84% of total wholesale revenue**, or close to 1% per customer on average.

Maintaining these relationships should be a business priority.

### Reactivation could recover meaningful revenue

Inactive customers had the highest average order value.

Winning back even a small number of high-value inactive accounts could create a meaningful revenue impact.

### Geography matters

Canada and Northwest appeared healthier because they combined strong revenue with a lower inactive-customer share.

The Southeast showed the greatest retention risk and should receive more targeted attention.

### Revenue is sensitive to Bike order volume

Bikes were purchased less frequently than Accessories and Components but generated a large share of revenue.

Because Bike orders are high-value and relatively infrequent, small changes in Bike order count can significantly affect overall revenue.

### Accessories and Components can stabilize revenue

Bikes can act as the main revenue anchor, while Accessories and Components can help increase order frequency between major Bike purchases.

Bundles and add-on strategies may help stabilize revenue and strengthen customer relationships.

## Recommendations

### 1. Prioritize the Top 10 Active customers

Actions could include:

* faster order processing
* priority customer support
* early access to new models
* preferred pricing
* expedited shipping
* quarterly account check-ins

The goal is to protect relationships with customers who already contribute a meaningful share of revenue.

---

### 2. Grow Bike orders

Set a target for the percentage of wholesale orders that include Bikes by store or territory.

Track this KPI regularly and identify territories where Bike sales have growth potential.

Possible actions:

* create Bike and Accessories bundles
* include Components with Bike purchases
* track product attach rate
* monitor average order value by category
* offer seasonal or model-specific promotions

---

### 3. Win back the Top 10 Inactive customers

Use targeted reactivation campaigns for previously high-value accounts.

Possible actions:

* time-limited discounts
* shipping credits
* installment plans for larger Bike orders
* service or upgrade campaigns
* seasonal Components and Accessories offers
* short outreach asking why the customer stopped ordering
* personalized offers based on previous purchase behavior

---

### 4. Focus on the Southeast

The Southeast had the highest inactive-customer share.

The business could compare this territory with healthier regions such as Canada and Northwest to understand differences in:

* sales-team practices
* customer communication
* shipping or fulfillment
* pricing
* product mix
* account-management frequency

Successful practices from stronger territories could then be tested in the Southeast.

---

## Next Steps for Analysis

### At-Risk customer funnel

Track how many At-Risk customers:

* return to Active status
* remain At-Risk
* become Inactive

This should be measured by territory and sales team.

### Product attach rates

Analyze where Accessories and Components can be sold together with Bikes.

Track:

* attach rate
* order frequency
* AOV by category
* revenue per customer

### Territory playbooks

Identify the customer-management practices used in Canada and Northwest.

Test similar approaches in the Southeast and monitor whether the inactive-customer share improves.

### Customer-level churn indicators

Explore whether declining order frequency, order size, or product variety can help identify customers before they become inactive.
